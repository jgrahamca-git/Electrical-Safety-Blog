### 1. Introduction & Context

In a fast-paced mining or industrial environment, the pressure to keep production running is immense. When a piece of equipment goes down, electricians are often pressured to implement a "quick fix" or "temporary run" to bypass the failed component and get the process back online. While temporary wiring is sometimes necessary for immediate troubleshooting or emergency operation, the danger arises when "temporary" becomes the new "permanent."

### 2. The Core Issue

Temporary wiring is, by definition, not designed for long-term use. It typically lacks the physical protection (conduit, cable tray) and environmental ratings required for harsh industrial settings.

- **Physical Damage:** Exposed cables laid across walkways, draped over pipes, or zip-tied to handrails are highly susceptible to mechanical damage from mobile equipment, falling debris, or personnel.
- **Environmental Degradation:** Standard extension cords or unprotected SO cord will rapidly degrade when exposed to the moisture, corrosive dust, oil, and extreme temperatures common in mining operations.
- **Overloading and Grounding Issues:** Temporary setups often bypass standard protection coordination and may lack proper equipment grounding conductors, increasing the risk of both fire and shock.
- **The "Out of Sight, Out of Mind" Trap:** Once the equipment is running again, the urgency to perform a proper, code-compliant repair vanishes. The temporary fix is forgotten until it eventually fails catastrophically.

### 3. Actionable Takeaways

- **Tag and Track:** Every temporary electrical modification must be logged, tagged with a defined expiration date, and assigned a work order for permanent repair. Treat that date as a hard deadline — "temporary" is a status with an end date, not a permanent installation method.
- **Physical Protection:** Even temporary wiring must be physically protected. Use heavy-duty cable ramps for floor crossings and avoid routing cables near sharp edges or heat sources.
- **Shift Handover Communication:** Temporary workarounds are a massive hazard for the next shift if they aren't communicated. Ensure all temporary wiring is explicitly mentioned during shift handover.
